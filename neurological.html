Advancing Neurological Research with Balance Mat

Exploring innovative approaches to assess neurological function, postural control, and neuropathy through advanced clinical research.

Research Collaboration

Balance Mat is being explored as a clinical research tool to investigate neurological conditions and measure changes associated with sensory and motor function. This research brings together expertise in neuroscience, neurology, and clinical neurophysiology to better understand human balance, nerve function, and neurological health.

Research Team

Leading clinical and academic expertise guiding this research.

Professor Vaughan Macefield

Professor of Neuroscience, Central Clinical School, Monash University

Professor Macefield is internationally recognised for his pioneering work in neuroscience, particularly for developing methodologies to record the firing properties of single, type-identified sympathetic neurones supplying muscle and skin.

More recently, he has developed advanced techniques for recording muscle sympathetic nerve activity (MSNA) simultaneously with functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) of the brain, providing new insight into the relationship between the nervous system and brain function.

Dr Branko Borojevic

Neuromuscular and Neurophysiology Fellow, Alfred Health

Dr Borojevic is a neurology and internal medicine trainee and researcher with specialist interests in neuromuscular disorders and clinical neurophysiology. His broader clinical interests include stroke medicine, general neurology, and medical education.

He has recently been using Balance Mat to assess people with neuropathy and investigate its potential role in neurological testing.
