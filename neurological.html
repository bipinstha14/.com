Advancing Neurological Research with the Neurometric Balance Mat

Exploring innovative approaches to assess neurological function, postural control and neuropathy through advanced clinical research.

Research Collaboration

The Neurometric Balance Mat is being explored as a valuable clinical research tool to investigate neurological conditions and measure changes associated with sensory and motor function.

This research brings together leading expertise in neuroscience, neurology and clinical neurophysiology to better understand human balance, nerve function and neurological health.

Professor Vaughan Macefield

Professor of Neuroscience, Central Clinical School, Monash University

Professor Vaughan Macefield is internationally recognised for his pioneering work in neuroscience, particularly for developing methodologies to record the firing properties of single, type-identified sympathetic neurones supplying muscle and skin.

More recently, he developed advanced techniques for recording muscle sympathetic nerve activity (MSNA) simultaneously with functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) of the brain, providing new insights into the relationship between the nervous system and brain function.

Dr Branko Borojevic

Neuromuscular and Neurophysiology Fellow, Alfred Health

Dr Branko Borojevic is a neurology and internal medicine trainee and researcher with specialist interests in neuromuscular disorders and clinical neurophysiology.

His broader clinical interests include stroke medicine, general neurology and medical education.

Branko has recently been using the Neurometric Balance Mat to assess people with neuropathy and investigate its potential role in neurological testing.
