Our Partners · Research & Academic Collaboration

University of Canberra

A research partner supporting the scientific validation, evidence development, and advancement of Balance Mat technology.

About the Partnership

In July 2021, Balance Mat Pty Ltd and the University of Canberra established a Collaborative Research Agreement to undertake a major research program focused on the development, evaluation, and validation of Balance Mat technology.

Through this collaboration, researchers from the University of Canberra have contributed to advancing the scientific understanding of balance measurement and postural sway assessment through a series of research activities and publications.

The partnership has produced multiple scientific outputs between 2022 and 2025, strengthening the evidence base behind Balance Mat and supporting its development as an objective, portable balance assessment technology.

Research Milestone: Technical and Clinical Validation

A significant milestone in the collaboration was the publication of a peer-reviewed study evaluating the technical and clinical performance of Balance Mat in older adults, comparing postural sway measurements against established balance assessment methods.

"Technical and Clinical Validation of a Portable Optical Fibre Balance Mat for Quantifying Postural Sway in Older Adults"
Authors: Abishek Shrestha, Damith Herath, Angie Fearon and Maryam Ghahramani
Published in: Sensors (2026)
Download the research paper (PDF) →

The research demonstrated the capability of Balance Mat to objectively quantify postural sway using a portable optical fibre-based system, supporting its potential application as an accessible balance assessment solution for healthcare and research environments.

Working Together

The collaboration between Balance Mat and the University of Canberra combines industry innovation with academic research expertise to generate scientific evidence and support the development of next-generation balance assessment technology.

Through this partnership, researchers have investigated the reliability, validity, and clinical applications of Balance Mat, helping establish a stronger foundation for future healthcare adoption and research applications.
