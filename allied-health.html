Objective Balance Assessment to Support Better Clinical Decisions

Balance is a key indicator of functional ability, mobility, independence, and overall wellbeing. Balance Mat provides a simple, repeatable, and objective approach to balance assessment — helping practitioners transform observations into measurable insights.

Book a Demonstration Request a Clinic Trial

Supporting Clinical Assessment and Decision-Making

Traditional balance assessments often rely heavily on clinician observation and patient feedback. While clinical expertise remains essential, objective measurements can provide additional information to support more informed decisions.

✅ Capture measurable balance performance data
✅ Establish individual baseline assessments
✅ Identify changes in balance ability over time
✅ Compare progress throughout rehabilitation
✅ Support personalised treatment planning

Falls Prevention and Mobility Management

Falls are a significant concern for older adults and individuals with mobility challenges. Early identification of balance decline allows practitioners to introduce targeted interventions before falls occur.

✅ Identify individuals with reduced balance stability
✅ Monitor changes in balance performance
✅ Develop targeted exercise and mobility programs
✅ Evaluate the effectiveness of interventions
✅ Support ongoing risk management strategies

Rehabilitation and Recovery Monitoring

Recovery after injury, surgery, illness, or neurological conditions requires ongoing assessment to understand whether a patient is improving.

📍

Before Treatment

  • Establish a baseline balance profile
  • Identify areas requiring intervention
  • Set measurable rehabilitation goals
📈

During Treatment

  • Monitor improvement over multiple sessions
  • Provide objective feedback to patients
  • Adjust exercise programs based on progress
✅

After Treatment

  • Evaluate functional improvements
  • Support discharge planning
  • Provide measurable outcomes

Supporting Neurological and Age-Related Conditions

Balance impairment can occur due to various conditions affecting coordination, strength, sensation, and movement control. Practitioners can use balance information alongside their clinical expertise to develop appropriate care strategies.

Reduced strength and stability
Neurological conditions
Post-injury rehabilitation

Deconditioning after hospitalisation

✅ Show patients measurable improvements
✅ Encourage participation in rehabilitation programs
✅ Increase confidence through visible progress tracking
✅ Support motivation and adherence to exercise plans
